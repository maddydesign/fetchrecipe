## You're not imagining it

Outline: Validate the reader's frustration. Recipe blogs really have gotten longer over the past decade. What used to be a quick list of ingredients and steps is now a 2,000-word essay.

## It's about Google, not the reader

Outline: Explain how Google's ranking algorithm favors longer content, more time on page, and "comprehensive" posts. Bloggers write long because short posts don't rank.

## Ad revenue plays a role too

Outline: More content means more ad slots. A recipe post with 2,000 words can fit 6-8 display ads. A 200-word post can fit maybe one.

## The "Jump to Recipe" button isn't enough

Outline: Many blogs now have a "Jump to Recipe" button, but it doesn't help with printing, saving, or removing the clutter. It's a band-aid.

## What you can do about it

Outline: Introduce FetchRecipe as a solution. Paste the URL, get just the recipe. Works with any recipe blog.
